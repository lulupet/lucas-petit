Create an account on Lichess
You can create an account here.
Why creating an online account at all?
Many people start playing chess with an application (against a computer) and actually, you can rise to a pretty serious level of chess by dpoing so but it can be a bit boring. When you play online, you can play against other people which makes it way more challenging, you can do tactics exercises, you can analyze your games, etc. Beside, you have an Elo rating that tells your level compared to other players so that your progression is measurable.
Why Lichess?
Many other websites exist. The most famous is chess.com but here are reasons why I prefer chess.com: - Lichess is free and will always be (you can give them some money so that they can keep existing but their main interest is not profitability). On chess.com You have to pay to get access to all of the features. - There is no adds on Lichess, there are if you don't pay on chess.com. - Lichess is developed by an open source community and chess.com is a company. - Lichess is French and chess.com is American.
Learn the rules
Pretty obvious, right?
Well, some people play dozens of chess games before fully understqnding how castling works and what the en passant move, so don't be like them and learn the rules from the beginning. Here is a checklist: - the moving of the pieces - castling - pawn promotion - en passant - check - checkmate - stalemate - the 50 moves rule - draw by insufficient material
Learn elementary checkmates
Alright, now you know that winning a game means checkmating your opponent so you have to know how to checkmate your opponent. There are a lot of ways to do so but some are fundamental and will often occur during your games: - the ladder mate - the back-rank mate - any mate that consists in a queen stuck to the opponent's king with a piece defending the queen
